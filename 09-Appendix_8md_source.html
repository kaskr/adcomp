11 |---------------------|------------------------------------------------------|

20 ## Profiling the inner problem
21 
22 This section describes the underlying theory of the argument `profile`
23 to `MakeADFun` intended to speedup and robustify linear mixed effect
24 models with a large number of fixed effects. With a few common model
25 properties (Assumption 1 and 2 below), which must be checked by the
26 user, one can apply the `profile` argument to move outer parameters to
27 the inner problem without affecting the model result.
28 
29 **Theorem 1 (Profiling inner problem)**
30 Assume that for any \f$\beta\f$ and \f$\theta\f$
31 
32 * **Assumption 1** The partial derivative \f$\partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta)\f$ is a linear function of u.
33 * **Assumption 2** The posterior mean is equal to the posterior mode: \f$E(u|x)=\hat u\f$
34 
35 Then the MLE
36 
37 \f[\hat \beta := \arg \max_{\beta} \left( \int \exp(-f(u,\beta,\theta)) \: du \right) \f]
38 
39 is a solution to the augmented system
40 
41 \f[
42 \begin{split}
43 \partial_{u} f(u,\beta,\theta) &= 0 \\
44 \partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta) &= 0
45 \end{split}
46 \f]
47 
48 The augmented system defines \f$\hat \beta\f$ implicitly as function of the posterior mode \f$\hat u\f$.
49 
50 *Proof*
51 
52 Differentiation of the negative log marginal likelihood gives
53 
54 \f[
55 \begin{split}
56 \partial_{\beta} \left( -\log \int \exp(-f(u,\beta,\theta)) \: du \right) &= E(\partial_{\beta}f(u,\beta,\theta) |x) \\
57 &= \partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta)_{|u=\hat u(\beta,\theta)}
58 \end{split}
59 \f]
60 
61 where the first equality holds in general and the second equality follows from assumptions (1) and (2).
62 
63 \f$\square\f$
64 
65 ### Example
66 
67 The standard situation for which **assumption 1** holds is when the
68 \f$\beta\f$s are the linear fixed effects of a mixed model. In this case
69 the joint negative log density takes the form
70 \f[ f(u,\beta,\theta) = \frac{1}{2}(u-A\beta)'\Sigma_{\theta}^{-1}(u-A\beta) + ... \f]
71 for some design matrix \f$A\f$ where ' \f$...\f$ ' does not depend on
72 \f$\beta\f$. The derivative
73 \f[ \partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta) = A'\Sigma_{\theta}^{-1}(u-A\beta) \f]
74 is thus a linear function of the random effect \f$u\f$.
75 
76 In general **assumption 2** holds exact for models with a symmetric
77 (e.g. Gaussian) posterior distribution.

79 ## Theory underlying sdreport
80 
81 This section supplements the documentation of `?sdreport` by adding
82 some missing details.
83 
84 As previously, we consider a general latent variable model with
85 parameter vector \f$\theta\f$, random effect vector \f$u\f$ and observation
86 vector \f$x\f$. The TMB estimation procedure works as follows:
87 
88 1. The MLE \f$\hat\theta=\hat\theta(x)\f$ is calculated and used as
89  estimator of \f$\theta\f$.
90 2. Denote by \f$\hat u(\theta,x)\f$ the random effect mode depending on
91  \f$\theta\f$ and \f$x\f$. Now, *plug in* the MLE, and we get our estimator
92  \f$\hat u\left(\hat\theta(x),x\right)\f$ of \f$u\f$.
93 
94 In general, we assume that \f$\hat\theta\f$ is a consistent estimator of
95 \f$\theta\f$. However, we do not in general require \f$\hat u\f$ to be
96 consistent for \f$u\f$. The purpose of sdreport is, for a given
97 realization of the pair \f$(u,x)\f$, to quantify the joint uncertainty of
98 \f$(\hat u,\hat\theta)\f$ as estimator of \f$(u,\theta)\f$. That is, we are
99 interested in the variance matrix of the difference
100 
101 \[D:=\begin{pmatrix}\hat u\left(\hat\theta(x),x\right) - u\\ \hat\theta(x) - \theta\end{pmatrix}\]
102 
103 An important point of the uncertainty quantification is to account
104 for plugging in \f$\hat\theta\f$ rather than using the true \f$\theta\f$.
105 
106 We calculate the variance using the standard formula:
107 
108 \[V[D]=E(V(D|x))+V(E(D|x))\]
109 
110 Consider \f$D\f$ conditionally on \f$x\f$. The second component does not
111 depend on \f$u\f$ and \f$\hat u\f$ is constant given \f$x\f$:
112 
113 \[V[D|x]=\begin{pmatrix}V[u|x] & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\]
114 
115 It follows that
116 
117 \[E(V[D|x])=\begin{pmatrix}E(V[u|x]) & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\]
118 
119 As central estimator of \f$E(V[u|x])\f$ we use \f$V[u|x]\f$ which is
120 approximated by the inverse random effect Hessian \f$H_{uu}^{-1}\f$ based
121 on the assumption that \f$u|x\f$ is well approximated by a Gaussian
122 distribution (a reasonable assumption given that we are using the
123 Laplace approximation). This explains the first term of variance formula in `?sdreport`:
124 
125 \[E(V[D|x]) \approx \begin{pmatrix} H_{uu}^{-1} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\]
126 
127 Likewise,
128 
129 \[E[D|x]=\begin{pmatrix}\hat u\left(\hat\theta(x),x\right) - E(u|x)\\ \hat\theta(x) - \theta\end{pmatrix}\]
130 
131 Again, asuming a Gaussian approximation of \f$u|x\f$, it follows that \f$E(u|x) \approx \hat u(\theta,x)\f$:
132 
133 \[E[D|x]=\begin{pmatrix}\hat u\left(\hat\theta(x),x\right) - \hat u(\theta,x)\\ \hat\theta(x) - \theta\end{pmatrix}\]
134 
135 We approximate the expectation using linerization of \f$\theta \rightarrow \hat u(\theta,x)\f$ around \f$\hat\theta(x)\f$
136 
137 \[E[D|x]=J_x \cdot (\hat\theta(x) - \theta)\]
138 
139 We now have the second term of the variance formula in `?sdreport`:
140 
141 \[V(E[D|x]) \approx J_x V(\hat\theta(x)) J_x'\]
142 
143 This term becomes negligible if the amount of data is high because of
144 the assumed asymptotic consistency of \f$\hat\theta\f$.
